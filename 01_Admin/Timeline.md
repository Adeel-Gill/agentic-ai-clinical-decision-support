# Timeline — 2026-07 to 2026-11

A realistic four-month plan to a submittable thesis and defense. Assumes part-time effort with
supervisor check-ins roughly every two weeks. Dates are targets, not deadlines; slip should be
absorbed by trimming the prototype scope, not the writing.

| Month | Window | Milestone | Key deliverables | Depends on |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Month 1 | 2026-07 | Literature + Ch. 1–2 locked | De-AI'd Ch. 1 & Ch. 2 with citations; taxonomy final; references verified; paper-ID reconciliation done | Style guide, References.bib |
| Month 2 | 2026-08 | Framework + data ready | Ch. 3 finalized (architecture, agents, HITL); MIMIC-IV cohort extracted; data dictionary confirmed | Ch. 2 gap; PhysioNet access |
| Month 3 | 2026-09 | Prototype + experiments | Bounded prototype runs; Ch. 4 evaluation executed (baselines, ablations, metrics); results tables | Ch. 3, cohort data |
| Month 4a | 2026-10 | Full draft | Ch. 4 write-up; Ch. 5 conclusions + RQ answers; front matter; internal review + revisions | Ch. 4 results |
| Month 4b | 2026-11 | Submission + defense | Final proofread; submit; defense deck complete; viva rehearsal and defense | Full draft, examiner report |

## Buffers and risks

- **PhysioNet credentialing / extraction delay** is the main schedule risk; if it slips, start the
  prototype against a synthetic or de-identified sample so Month 3 is not blocked.
- **Prototype scope** is the primary release valve: keep it bounded to what the Chapter 4 evaluation
  needs. Do not expand it at the cost of the write-up.
- Two weeks of total slack are folded across Months 3–4; protect the Month 4b defense window.
