# Scope of Research

This research focuses on designing a conceptual Agentic AI framework for intelligent patient monitoring and clinical decision support. The study emphasizes architectural design and framework development rather than full-scale deployment within hospital information systems.

The proposed framework integrates multiple Agentic AI capabilities, including autonomous reasoning, memory management, Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), planning, and multi-agent collaboration. The architecture is designed around the publicly available MIMIC-IV dataset, which contains anonymized electronic health records collected from intensive care units.

The framework considers structured and unstructured healthcare information, including patient demographics, admissions, laboratory results, vital signs, medications, diagnoses, procedures, and clinical notes. Human oversight is incorporated through a clinician validation layer to ensure that AI-generated recommendations assist rather than replace physician decision making.

The implementation of production-ready hospital software, integration with live Electronic Health Record systems, and clinical validation through real patient trials are outside the scope of this research. Instead, the study provides a comprehensive conceptual framework that can guide future implementation and evaluation.
