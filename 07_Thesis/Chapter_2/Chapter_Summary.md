# 2.10 Chapter Summary

This chapter reviewed the literature related to Large Language Models (LLMs), Agentic AI, Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), multi-agent systems, and healthcare artificial intelligence. The review began by introducing the evolution of LLMs and discussing their increasing role in intelligent healthcare applications. It then examined the emergence of Agentic AI and the architectural components that enable autonomous reasoning, planning, memory management, and tool utilization.

Several influential agent frameworks, including ReAct, AutoGen, CAMEL, MetaGPT, Toolformer, and Generative Agents, were analyzed to understand how intelligent agents perform complex tasks through reasoning and collaboration. The chapter also reviewed Retrieval-Augmented Generation and its importance in improving factual accuracy by integrating external medical knowledge with language models. In addition, healthcare-specific AI systems such as Med-PaLM, MedAgents, MedRAG, Clinical Camel, Agent Hospital, and Med-PaLM M were examined to understand current developments in intelligent clinical decision support.

The review highlighted that although existing research has achieved significant progress in autonomous reasoning, knowledge retrieval, and collaborative agent systems, several challenges remain unresolved. These include limited integration of persistent memory, insufficient coordination among specialized agents, lack of unified frameworks for intelligent patient monitoring, concerns regarding hallucinations, and the need for transparent and trustworthy AI systems that support clinicians in real-world healthcare environments.

Based on the analysis of the selected literature, a clear research gap was identified. Current approaches generally address individual components of Agentic AI but rarely integrate reasoning, memory, Retrieval-Augmented Generation, multi-agent orchestration, and Human-in-the-Loop validation within a single healthcare framework. This gap motivates the development of the proposed Agentic AI Framework for Intelligent Patient Monitoring and Clinical Decision Support using the MIMIC-IV dataset.

The findings presented in this chapter establish the theoretical and technical foundation for the proposed research. Building upon these insights, the next chapter describes the research methodology, proposed system architecture, implementation strategy, dataset preparation, and evaluation approach that will be used to design and validate the proposed Agentic AI framework.
